English Masterclass 📚

✅ Parts of Speech

  • Noun: Name of person, place, thing (e.g., Ali, Karachi).
  • Pronoun: Used instead of noun (e.g., He, She, It).
  • Verb: Action word (e.g., Run, Eat).
  • Adjective: Describes noun (e.g., Good, Red).
  • Adverb: Describes verb (e.g., Quickly, Slowly).
  • Preposition: Shows relationship (e.g., In, On, At).
  • Conjunction: Connects words (e.g., And, But).
  • Interjection: Expression of emotion (e.g., Wow!, Ouch!).

✅ Basic Tenses (Start of 12)

Present Simple: I play cricket. (Daily Habit)

Past Simple: I played cricket. (Finished Action)
Future Simple: I will play cricket. (Tomorrow)

✅ Sentence Structure

Subject + Verb + Object

Ali eats mango.

✅ Articles

A: Used before consonant sounds (A cat).

An: Used before vowel sounds (An apple).

The: Used for specific things (The sun).

✅ Basic Prepositions

In (box), On (table), At (school), Under (chair), Behind (tree).

🔹 Intermediate Level: All 12 Tenses Detail

Present Continuous: I am playing. (Right now)

Present Perfect: I have played. (Just finished)

Past Continuous: I was playing. (Was going on)

Past Perfect: I had played before he came.

Future Continuous: I will be playing tomorrow.

Future Perfect: I will have played by 5 PM.

✅ Modal Verbs

Can (Ability), Could (Past ability), May (Permission), Might (Possibility), Must (Obligation), Should (Advice).

✅ Active & Passive Voice

Active: Ali wrote a letter. (Focus on Doer)

Passive: A letter was written by Ali. (Focus on Object)

✅ Direct & Indirect Speech

Direct: He said, “I am tired.”

Indirect: He said that he was tired.

✅ Conditional Sentences

Type 1: If you study, you will pass. (Real possibility)

Type 2: If I were rich, I would buy a car. (Unreal)

🔹 Advanced Level

Yeh level fluent aur professional English ke liye hota hai.

✅ Complex & Compound Sentences

Compound: I like coffee, but my brother likes tea.

Complex: Although it was raining, we went outside.

✅ Relative Clauses

Connecting two sentences using Who, Which, That.

The boy who is playing is my brother.

✅ Gerunds & Infinitives

Gerund (Verb+ing as noun): I enjoy reading.

Infinitive (To + Verb): I want to learn English.

✅ Subjunctive Mood

Used for wishes or hypothetical situations.

I wish I were rich. (Not 'was')

✅ Phrasal Verbs

  • Give up: Chorna (Quit)
  • Look after: Dekhbhal karna (Take care)
  • Run into: Achanak milna (Meet by accident)
